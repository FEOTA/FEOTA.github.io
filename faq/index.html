Frequently Asked Questions

 

Is IELTS required to apply in the Finnish universities?

– Yes, Finnish universities require an overall band score of 6.5 for IELTS. All of them accept IELTS Academic test while some universities also accept IELTS Indicator test. However, IELTS General test is not accepted for university level studies.

How much is the application fee to Finnish universities?

– There is no application fee to the Finnish universities. However, for non-EU students, there is an annual tuition fee and student union fee which differs from one university to the other.

Do I need to know Finnish language to obtain a student visa in Finland?

– No, Finnish language is not a must to apply for a student visa (B-permit) in Finland. Your studies in Finnish universities can be completed entirely in English.

Am I guaranteed an admission to a Finnish university if I buy FEOTA’s consultancy service?

– Top Finnish universities are very selective and accept only few applicants every year who demonstrate strong motivation and have sufficient educational background to be able to complete the selected degree programmes. Some degree programmes are harder to get into compared to others and it is completely upto the university admission services to make the final decision. FEOTA does not in any way involve or interfere with university’s selection process. Instead, we aim to help you prepare to the best of your abilities so that your chance of admittance will be higher.

Is it possible to get Finnish citizenship while studying in Finland?

– To obtain a Finnish citizenship, one must have sufficient Finnish language skills, means of support, clean criminal record, and sufficient period of residence in Finland. The student visa or B permit is considered a temporary permit and is not enough to fulfil the requirement of sufficient period of residency condition. This means you must have a continuous residence permit for a certain period before you are eligibe to apply for Finnish citizenship.
