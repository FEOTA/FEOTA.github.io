Finland

A well-hidden gem of the North

Heard about Finland before?

Finland is one of the world’s most northern countries located in Northern Europe. It is also the most densely forested country in Europe with more than 75% of its land area covered in forest. Surrounded by Russia, Sweden, Norway, Gulf of Finland and the Gulf of Bothnia, Finland is home to 5.5 million people.

Other than the legendary Nokia, Finland is also best known for being the happiest country in the world. The country takes pride in its vast number of lakes, a total of about 188,000 lakes present that rightfully earned Finland its nickname ‘the land of a thousand lakes’. Some of the well-known tourists attractions in Finland include Santa Claus Village, Suomenlinna Fortress, Uspensky Orthodox Cathedral, Temppeliaukio Church, Åland Archipelago, Turku Castle, Lake Saimaa, Yyteri Beach, Rovaniemi and the Arctic and many more.

If you are wondering which country to choose for higher education, look no further because Finland has so much to offer!
